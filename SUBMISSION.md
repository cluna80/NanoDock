## Long Description (min 100 words)
Use the contents of `README.md` verbatim, or the condensed version below:

NanoDock demonstrates the Agentic Economy with a working drug-discovery compute marketplace. A user (or another AI agent) submits a SMILES molecule and target protein, paying $0.01 USDC as a Circle Nanopayment. Inside the server, an OrchestratorAgent fans out internal paid calls to a DockingAgent ($0.005), ADMETAgent ($0.002), and ValidatorAgent ($0.001), keeping a $0.002 gross margin. Every agent has its own Circle Wallet and an ERC-8004 Identity NFT on Arc. After every successful screening, the buyer posts a reputation feedback entry on the ERC-8004 Reputation Registry scored by real output quality (binding affinity, Lipinski compliance, ChEMBL novelty). A Gemini 3 orchestrator drives the whole pipeline in natural language via Function Calling.

One /screen call generates 5 onchain transactions (4 payments + 1 reputation). A 25-molecule demo run generates 125+ txns. The margin story is the entire point: at Ethereum L1 gas (~$0.50/tx), a 10,000-molecule screening campaign costs ~$20,000 in gas against $100 of revenue — 200× underwater. On Arc with Nanopayments batching through Circle Gateway, the same campaign settles at ~$0.60 in amortized gas against $100 revenue, with real gross profit flowing autonomously between agents that have portable, publicly-queryable reputation scores.

## Circle Product Feedback
See "Circle Product Feedback" section in README.md. Key points:
1. USDC contract address discovery friction on Arc testnet docs
2. EIP-3009 domain separator values not documented in one place
3. Need for a single canonical TypeScript `@circle/nanopayments` SDK
4. Browser-only faucet UX (CLI/API would help hackathon scripts)
5. Gateway deposit vs ERC-20 transfer mental model clarification
6. Arcscan could surface EIP-3009 events more prominently
7. x402 facilitator endpoint discovery could be in a well-known URL
